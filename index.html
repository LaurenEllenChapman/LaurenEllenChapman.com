Lauren Chapman

Web. Print. Broadcast. Design.

Hey beautiful person! Welcome to my portfolio. My name is Lauren Chapman and I am a student journalist at Ball State University. I'm from Indianapolis, and do a little bit of everything.

I got into journalism because my high school counselor wouldn't let me take the class I wanted to. She told me to take the newspaper class. I told her I thought journalism was stupid and fluffy. Three years later, I was the second runner-up for the Indiana High School Journalist of the Year.

I went to Ball State because they gave me awards while I was reporting in high school. The education I received there gave me a well-rounded approach to storytelling. I worked as a print and radio reporter. I spent a long time as a designer.

Recently, my passion has been for online design and interactive content. No matter what, my passion is storytelling. This almost manic desire to tell people stories and share information led me to learning different methods like audio, video, photography, print and design.

TIMELINE: Ebola in the U.S.

I wrote a column about the fear surrounding Ebola in the United States, but I wanted interactive content with it. After searching through the Associated Press, I found the structure of a timeline and added on to it as more cases appeared in the United States.



Using the open source tool Timeline JS, I created a way for people to interact with content.

QUIZ: What Village Bar are you?

I wanted to develop a quiz that would generate reader engagement. It was a raging success. I had more than 2,000 hits on the page. Even better, it generated viewership online during the weekend, when we don't normal get a lot of traffic.


Because of this quiz, we have been able to expand this tool into content promotion. During Homecoming, a quiz about the school's fight song linked to news content.

NEWS: Bicyclists headed to Davis can get free train tickets back to Sacramento

I wanted to do a little bit of newswriting in addition to the digital work that I was doing in Sacramento. This story came out of a press release from a community newsletter. After tracing these businesses in Davis, I ended up with a great story about a guy who wanted to ride his bike.

John Swann used to commute regularly by bike from Davis to his job in Sacramento, but he'd often get a ride home.

"It's a tough commute," said the semi-retired Swann, who left a software job two years ago and now owns an electric-bicycle business in Davis.

Looking for a way to get people out of their cars and reduce what can easily be a 40-mile round trip between the cities, he collaborated with other Davis business owners and the Yolo County Visitors Bureau.

SGA PRESIDENT RESIGNS

After making racially insensitive tweets about Asian people, our student government president resigned at 5:15 p.m. Our news meeting started at 5 p.m. Within two minutes of resigning, we had a completely new art for package and someone writing a 1,000 word story.

This front page won several awards for the paper, including Indiana Collegiate Press Association and Society of Professional Journalists.
